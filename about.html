About Spider-Man

The origin story of your friendly neighborhood hero

Peter Benjamin Parker

Real Name: Peter Benjamin Parker
Occupation: Photographer, Student, Superhero
Location: Queens, New York City
First Appearance: Amazing Fantasy #15 (1962)
Created by: Stan Lee & Steve Ditko

Origin Story

The Spider Bite

Peter Parker was an ordinary high school student living with his Aunt May and Uncle Ben in Queens, New York. During a science exhibition, Peter was bitten by a radioactive spider that had been exposed to radiation. This bite would change his life forever.

Initially unaware of the changes happening to his body, Peter soon discovered he had gained incredible powers: superhuman strength, agility, the ability to cling to walls, and an amazing "spider-sense" that warned him of danger.

Great Responsibility

At first, Peter used his powers for personal gain, appearing on television and in wrestling matches. However, his selfishness would lead to tragedy when he failed to stop a burglar who later killed his beloved Uncle Ben.

This devastating loss taught Peter the most important lesson of his life: "With great power comes great responsibility." From that moment forward, Peter dedicated his life to protecting others as the amazing Spider-Man.

The Web-Slinger

Using his scientific knowledge, Peter created web-shooters and a distinctive red and blue costume. As Spider-Man, he began fighting crime in New York City, facing villains like the Green Goblin, Doctor Octopus, and Venom.

Despite the challenges of balancing his personal life with his superhero duties, Peter continues to protect the innocent and uphold the values his Uncle Ben taught him.

Powers & Abilities

Superhuman Strength

Spider-Man possesses superhuman strength, enabling him to lift approximately 10 tons under optimal conditions. This strength extends to his legs, allowing him to leap great distances.

Wall-Crawling

Perhaps his most famous ability, Spider-Man can adhere to walls and ceilings through molecular forces, allowing him to scale any surface regardless of its angle or material.

Spider-Sense

An extrasensory ability that warns Spider-Man of approaching danger. This "sixth sense" has saved his life countless times and gives him an edge in combat.

Enhanced Agility

Spider-Man's agility, balance, and bodily coordination are enhanced to superhuman levels, making him incredibly acrobatic and able to dodge attacks with ease.

Accelerated Healing

While not as dramatic as some heroes, Spider-Man heals faster than normal humans and has increased resistance to injury and disease.

Genius Intellect

Peter Parker is naturally gifted with a genius-level intellect, particularly in chemistry, physics, and engineering, which he uses to create his web-shooters and other gadgets.
